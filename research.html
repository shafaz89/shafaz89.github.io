Research

Bacterial spectrins

My research focuses on staphylococcal cell biology, with particular emphasis on the multifaceted roles of bacterial spectrins in cell division, envelope biosynthesis, DNA replication, and teichoic acids biosynthesis. We are investigating how spectrins function as structural scaffolds that precisely orient the Z-ring to ensure orthogonal cell division, as well as their role as molecular organizers of the septal membrane, coordinating complex processes at the site of division.

The accessory Sec pathway

We are exploring the staphylococcal accessory Sec pathway, with a focus on the glycosylation mediated by its specialized components. Our studies aim to elucidate how these glycosylation events influence staphylococcal physiology and contribute to virulence.
